The problem · money

A clinic that feels flat-out is quietly losing money it never sees.

Charges get written off rather than re-billed. Some clinics run dedicated staff just to check invoices before discharge — and still find A$100–400/month in errors by hand. Phones never stop, slots go empty, recalls lapse.

None of it shows up as a line item on the P&L. Which is exactly why it never gets fixed.

The four leaks

Four leaks. Every clinic. Invisible on the P&L.

5–10%Missed chargesHover / tapOf total revenue never billed — line items forgotten in a packed day. AAHA finds 17% of lab tests go unbilled.
~25%Missed callsHover / tapOf inbound calls go unanswered; most callers never ring back. New clients walk to the next clinic.
10–20%No-showsHover / tapOf booked slots lost, with no waitlist to backfill. Every empty slot is fixed cost at zero revenue.
#1Lapsed recallHover / tapThe most under-used revenue lever. Reminders tied to invoicing mean an unbilled service also drops out of recall.
What that adds up to

Every clinic is leaving thousands on the table — every month.

A$4,000–8,000 Recoverable per clinic, every month, across the four leaks. That is the number Vera is paid a share of — so the product sells on results, not promises.
What Vera is

An AI scribe that pays for itself — then makes the practice money it already earned.

It records the consult with consent, writes accurate notes back into the clinic’s existing software, and — as it listens — flags the billable items that never made it onto the invoice.

Risks & mitigations

The five ways this fails — and the decision against each.

Scribe commoditisation & free tiers
Treat the scribe as customer acquisition, not the product. Monetise the recovery layer a free scribe can’t match.
PIMS incumbents build it themselves
Move fast and stay cross-PIMS — the benefit without the rip-and-replace their own features can’t be.
Charge-capture accuracy & liability
Human-in-the-loop by design — Vera suggests, never auto-bills. Every suggestion traces to the source utterance.
Consent & recording law
All-party consent everywhere, timestamped in-app, with Australian data residency. Detailed next.
SMB churn
Value-based lock-in — a clinic making A$4–8k/month from recovery doesn’t cancel; removing Vera re-opens the leaks.
Compliance by design

All-party consent, everywhere, by design.

  • Scripted verbal consent from the owner at the start of every recorded consult, timestamped in an in-app log.
  • One-tap decline — “client declined, don’t record” — with unchanged standard of care.
  • Australian data residency, encryption in transit and at rest, clear retention & deletion policy.
  • Privacy Act & OAIC-aligned — a privacy impact assessment; no training on client audio without explicit consent.
  • The vet stays the accountable author — Vera proposes, the clinician reviews and signs.
  • Full auditability — every note and charge suggestion traces back to the source utterance.

One clean workflow works in all eight Australian jurisdictions — it satisfies the strictest all-party states and moots the one-party rules.
